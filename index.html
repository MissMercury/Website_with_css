Data Scientist / Programmer

Hello.

I did my thesis at the functional biology lab at the KU Leuven. Here I worked with bacteria and yeast (Saccharomyces cerevisiae) and used a broad range of techniques including PCR, genetical manipulation of yeast, cloning, and western blot. I really enjoyed my time here and I was passionate about the research. After this, I started working at the Stem cell institute at the KU Leuven. Here I had the opportunity to have the freedom to plan experiments and I had the chance to broaden my knowledge in cell culture, molecular biology, animal experiments, data analysis etc. During this last year, I moved to the University of Edinburgh. Here I started working with yeast again. Here, I got the chance of using my programming skills .I have more than eight years of experience in the academic world, working with master students, PhD students and post- docs.

My Skills.

Laboratory skills

In the more than eight years I spent in the lab, I learned a lot of different techniques. Here are some of them:

Cell culture: yeast, bacterial, transformation, cloning, growthcurve, viral, mammalian, Primary cell line, Single cell isolation, FACS sorting, iPSC
Animal In Vivo experiments: injections, dissections, conventional and SPF management
Animal Ex Vivo experiments: cryosectioning, microtome, muscle strength measuring system
Staining: Histological and immunofluorescent staining
Imaging: Bright field and fluorescent microscopy, Bioluminescent imaging
Molecular biology: Plasmid, RNA, DNA and protein extraction, PCR, qRT-PCR, Western Blot, SmartSeq2, RNASeq, Elisa
Data Analysis: Sequencing, FACS, bioinformatics
Training: bachelor, master and PhD students

Data science and programming
